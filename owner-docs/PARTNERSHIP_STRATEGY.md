## Partnership Types

### Technology Partners
**Goal:** Integrate services, expand capabilities

**Targets:**
- AI providers (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google)
- Cloud infrastructure (AWS, Vercel, Supabase)
- Payment processors (Stripe, PayPal)
- Marketing tools (Mailchimp, HubSpot)
- Analytics platforms (Google, Mixpanel)

**Benefits:**
- Reduced costs through partnership programs
- Technical support
- Co-marketing opportunities
- Early access to features
- Platform credibility

**Status:** 5 active (OpenAI, Stripe, PayPal, Vercel, Supabase)

### Distribution Partners
**Goal:** Access their customer base

**Targets:**
- Marketing agencies (resell to clients)
- Educational institutions (student access)
- Business incubators (startup tools)
- Franchise systems (standardized creative)
- Professional associations (member benefit)

**Model:**
- Revenue share (70/30 split)
- White-label licensing
- Affiliate commissions
- Bulk licensing

**Priority:** Marketing agencies, universities

### Strategic Integrations
**Goal:** Seamless workflow for users

**Targets:**
- CRM systems (Salesforce, HubSpot)
- Project management (Asana, Monday)
- Cloud storage (Google Drive, Dropbox)
- Communication (Slack, Teams)
- eCommerce (Shopify, WooCommerce)

**Implementation:**
- API connections
- OAuth integration
- Webhook triggers
- Plugin/app marketplace

**Roadmap:** Q2 2026

### Content Partners
**Goal:** Provide resources, build library

**Targets:**
- Stock photo services
- Font foundries
- Music libraries
- Template creators
- Educational content

**Models:**
- Revenue share on usage
- Exclusive licensing
- Co-branded content
- Affiliate arrangements

## Partnership Process

### Phase 1: Identification
1. Research potential partners
2. Evaluate fit and value
3. Prioritize by impact
4. Create target list
5. Assign DRI (Directly Responsible Individual)

### Phase 2: Outreach
1. Craft personalized pitch
2. Highlight mutual benefits
3. Share traction/metrics
4. Request intro call
5. Follow up persistently

### Phase 3: Negotiation
1. Understand their goals
2. Propose win-win structure
3. Define success metrics
4. Draft partnership agreement
5. Legal review

### Phase 4: Onboarding
1. Technical integration
2. Marketing alignment
3. Training and resources
4. Launch plan
5. Success metrics

### Phase 5: Management
1. Regular check-ins
2. Track performance
3. Optimize and iterate
4. Expand scope
5. Renewal discussions

## Partnership Agreements

### Standard Terms
- **Duration:** 12 months, auto-renew
- **Termination:** 90 days notice
- **Exclusivity:** Non-exclusive
- **IP Rights:** Each party retains own
- **Confidentiality:** 2 years post-termination

### Revenue Share Model
- **Standard:** 70% us / 30% partner
- **Strategic:** 60% us / 40% partner
- **Enterprise:** Negotiable

### SLA Requirements
- **Uptime:** 99.9%
- **Support response:** 24 hours
- **Data processing:** GDPR/CCPA compliant
- **Security:** SOC 2 compliance
